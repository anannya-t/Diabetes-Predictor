FAQ

  1. What do the numbers 0, 1, and 2 mean?

    2. They represent different types of diabetes. 0 means no risk for diabetes, 1 means risk for type 1 diabetes, and 2 means risk for type 2 diabetes.

    The more often a 1 or 2 appears in the results, the higher the risk of the user developing diabetes in the future.

  2. What factors are used in the model?

    3. The factors we used are height, weight, age, sex, history of smoking, high cholesterol, and heart disease.

  3. What data is the model based on?

    4. We used a data set from Kaggle (BRFSS 2015)

    Disclaimer: Do not rely on this tool completely! There could be many other factors that contribute to diabetes that this model does not cover.
